## Description

Background

  


Romance scams (sometimes called “pig butchering”) aren’t just fraud - they’re human trafficking operations. Behind countless fraudulent cryptocurrency investment schemes that drain a victim’s wallet, there are often trafficked workers inside fortified compounds across Southeast Asia, forced to run the scam under threat of violence. The crypto industry talks about protecting users from fraud. I’m working to dismantle the infrastructure that makes the fraud possible in the first place.

  


I’m Joe “dobs” Dobson - a security researcher and incident responder with over 20 years of experience, including work with Mandiant, Google, and the US Military. I have been doing pro-bono work at the intersection of human trafficking and cryptocurrency fraud: investigating scam compound operations, helping secure anti-trafficking NGOs, and tracing the on-chain infrastructure that these networks rely on. None of this work is paid. I do it because the problem is urgent, the crypto community is uniquely positioned to help, and not enough folks with the right skills are showing up.

  


I’ve conducted firsthand field research at Myanmar-based scam compound sites - including KK Park, Tai Chang, and Shwe Kokko - documenting how these operations work on the ground and presenting findings to the Web3 security community at conferences like darkMode (SEAL). I also participated in the recent documentary from ChainPatrol, “Lights In Dark Rooms”.

  


This work supporting NGOs on the front lines - from cybersecurity best practices to cryptocurrency investigations - has been self-funded and pro-bono.

_Close-up views of two scam compounds in Myanmar._

  


What I do

  


My work spans three areas that are rarely connected but need to be:

  


**On-chain investigation** \- Tracing fraud infrastructure across blockchains, identifying wallet clusters, mapping laundering flows, and linking on-chain activity back to the compound operations behind it.

  


**NGO security -** Anti-trafficking organizations operate in dangerous environments with limited resources and are frequent targets themselves. These groups are doing incredible work on tiny budgets. I help these groups harden their infrastructure, improve their operational security, and protect the people doing rescue and advocacy work on the ground.

  


**Detection research** \- I’m establishing techniques that identify patterns specific to compound-run romance scam operations, methods I share carefully to build awareness without tipping off operators. The goal is to turn these into tools and intelligence that exchanges and protocols can act on.

  


What funding supports

  


Every dollar raised goes directly toward this pro-bono work:

  


**Research and investigation** \- Deep-dive analysis of fraud campaigns, wallet forensics, attribution work, and tracking compound operations as they adapt and relocate.

  


**Open-source tooling** \- Building and releasing detection and analysis tools that help the broader Web3 security community identify and respond to trafficking-linked fraud infrastructure.

  


**Field work** \- Travel to affected regions to gather ground-truth intelligence, build relationships with local partners, and validate on-chain findings against physical operations.

  


**NGO support** \- Hands-on security assessments and hardening for anti-trafficking organizations that can’t afford to hire help.

  


How funding would be spent:

  


$10k and less: Building and releasing some detection and analysis tools to assist with identifying and tracking fraudulent trading platforms and investment scam infrastructure. This would be geared towards feeding data into tools such as EthPhishingDetect to warn cryptocurrency users before visiting such malicious sites.

  


There is a lot that can be done in this range - if possible, I would also build automated processes to flag malicious cryptocurrency job ads that are used to recruit and traffick victims.

  


$10k-$25k: This supports field work/on-the-ground investigations and security work for NGOs. This includes supporting debriefs of trafficking victims to understand how cryptocurrency was used by their captors, what platforms were used, and informing how to trace it.

  


Investigatory work also includes in-person meetings to unmask their on-chain activity, determine offramps, and work with local authorities to cut the flow of funds.

  


While in the local areas, I will meet with NGOs to assist them in growing their cybersecurity posture - from educating them on passkeys to hunting for malicious activity in their networks.

  


$50k+: On top of everything else, this helps to secure NGOs and provide safety to their personnel. I would sponsor/hire someone in SE Asia - likely a refugee from Myanmar living in Thailand - to assist with in-person research and work. I would personally mentor this individual, sharing my 20+ years of cybersecurity experience with them so they can convey it in-person in local languages with NGO workers.

  


Why this matters to Web3

  


This problem is a direct threat to crypto’s legitimacy. Tens of billions in stolen funds flow through DeFi protocols and centralized exchanges, and behind those flows are real people - both the victims who lose their savings and the trafficked individuals forced to execute the scams. The Web3 community has the tools, the talent, and the on-chain transparency to make a meaningful difference here. I’m asking you to fund the work that connects those capabilities to impact.
