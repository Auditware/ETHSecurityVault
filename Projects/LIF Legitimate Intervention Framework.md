## Description

**LIF (Legitimate Intervention Framework)** is an open-source Ethereum security research and tooling project maintained by Parametrig. It helps protocols design credible incident response before they are in crisis.

  


When exploits happen, outcomes are shaped by what follows, more than the vulnerability: who can act, under what authority, how quickly, and with what constraints.

  


In practice, emergency interventions already happen across Ethereum and its L2 ecosystem (pauses, upgrades, validator or bridge coordination, negotiations, recovery actions), but they are often improvised. That creates governance risk and legitimacy disputes exactly when systems are under maximum stress.

  


LIF turns incident response into a design problem. We provide a maintained dataset of exploit and intervention cases, a taxonomy that makes response actions comparable (scope, authority, timing, outcomes), and practical playbooks and templates that protocols can adopt to pre-commit to bounded emergency powers and auditable recovery procedures. The goal is to make emergency authority explicit, constrained, and reviewable.

  


This work also supports the **next phase of crypto protection:** making risk legible enough for **insurance** and recovery mechanisms to be priced and executed credibly. Protocols are not equally insurable. A protocol with clear circuit breakers, monitoring, and a bounded response plan should not be treated the same as one that has none. LIF helps the ecosystem standardize these guardrails so incident response improves and coverage boundaries become clearer over time.

  


Donations will fund LIF as a public good by:

  1. shipping a **readiness and insurability toolkit** that protocols can use to improve their incident response posture before an incident
  2. upgrading the [dataset](https://lif-research.org/database) into a verification-grade release process (sources, confidence, changelogs)
  3. publishing adoptable incident response and emergency governance templates
  4. expanding L2 and cross-domain response case coverage
  5. building open tooling on [lif-research.org](lif-research.org) to make precedent searchable and usable under time pressure.
