## Description

Security knowledge in crypto is scattered everywhere - blog posts, Twitter threads, Discord channels, tribal knowledge locked inside teams. Every new project reinvents the wheel, auditors lack consistent baselines, and newcomers face a brutal learning curve. We think the ecosystem deserves better.

  


SEAL Frameworks is a SEAL (Security Alliance) Initiative - SEAL is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit founded by samczsun that serves as neutral security infrastructure for crypto.

  


We've built an open-source, community-driven collection of security playbooks covering the full spectrum of what it takes to secure a Web3 project. over two dozen frameworks published so far - operational security, incident management, multisig operations, wallet security, infrastructure, security testing, community management, insider threats (including DPRK IT workers), ENS security, and more.

  


These aren't rigid rulebooks. They're modular, practical starting points that evolve as the ecosystem learns. dozens of contributors have shaped the frameworks so far, including independent security researchers, auditors, and teams at leading protocols.

  


SEAL Frameworks is also the technical backbone behind other SEAL initiatives. Our incident management playbooks come directly from hundreds of SEAL 911 emergency responses. Our multisig and operational security guides inform SEAL Certifications criteria. Our Safe Harbor framework gives whitehats the guidance they need to act responsibly during rescues.

  


Our mission is to make SEAL Frameworks the definitive open-source security reference for crypto - so that no team has to start from zero, and every builder has access to battle-tested best practices regardless of budget or team size.

  


Your donation goes directly toward SEAL Frameworks - supporting the contributors who write and maintain the frameworks and the tooling that keeps our work going: framework authoring, open-source repo maintenance, contributor working groups, and keeping published frameworks up to date with real-world lessons from SEAL 911 and the wider ecosystem. The biggest line item is operating costs for the researchers, auditors, and authors doing the work.

  


Note: SEAL Frameworks is an initiative of SEAL (Security Alliance), a 501(c)(3) nonprofit. Donations made through this Giveth project stay with SEAL Frameworks and support the contributors and infrastructure dedicated to this initiative - they are not pooled across SEAL's other initiatives.
