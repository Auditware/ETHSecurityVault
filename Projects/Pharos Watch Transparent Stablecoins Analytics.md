## Description

Stablecoin failures are among the most systemic risks in the Ethereum ecosystem. When a stablecoin loses its peg, the damage ripples across DeFi protocols, liquidity pools, and user funds simultaneously, often across multiple chains and L2s at once. Most users never see it coming because the tools available to them only show price. They do not show structure.

  


Pharos is an open-source stablecoin security analytics platform built to change that. Where most dashboards tell you whether a peg is holding, Pharos tells you what is holding it up, how fragile the exit really is, and where the risk sits before the surface starts to crack.

  


The platform aggregates on-chain data from integrated APIs including DefiLlama, Curve, CoinGecko, and others, to produce a comprehensive security profile for every stablecoin it tracks. This includes collateral composition, custody risk, blacklist and censorship exposure, liquidity depth, redemption path analysis, and a proprietary Depeg Early Warning System (DEWS) that monitors stress signals in real time across all chains.

Pharos currently tracks over 150 stablecoins across all major chains and L2s, including non-USD pegged assets systematically overlooked by existing security tooling. It serves over 2,000 users ranging from individual researchers to protocol teams and treasury managers.

  


The platform's real-world impact is already measurable. Pharos flagged the USR depeg risk early before the exploit became public, demonstrating that its monitoring infrastructure can surface systemic risk before it becomes a crisis.

  


Pharos is free at its base layer and fully open source. It is infrastructure built on the conviction that stablecoin transparency is a prerequisite for a safer Ethereum ecosystem, and that the people who need it most should never have to pay to access it.

The stablecoin market has grown into a critical layer of Ethereum's monetary infrastructure. Pharos is built to keep it honest.
