## Description

Hi, my name is Vladimir but I am wider known as Officer CIA or Officer’s Notes. Operational security, OSINT, and DeFi are my primary areas of expertise and concentration. Having worked in cybersecurity for the past ten years, I am now totally [focused](https://github.com/OffcierCia/DeFi-Developer-Road-Map) on blockchain security, especially investigating new attack types and doing some on-chain [research](https://x.com/officer_secret/status/2043406886968455551?s=46).

  


I conduct operations security audits for my clients, who are frequently the targets of assaults because of their involvement in the financial and crypto industries. I was also the first one to purpose an on-chain defence technology.

  


This project will allow me to write more OpSec-themed articles both for projects & teams. 

  


**Building a Secure Web3 Ecosystem Through Knowledge**

  


In the decentralized world, a single operational slip-up — a leaked seed phrase, insecure team communications, or overlooked social-engineering vector — can wipe out years of work and millions in value. Yet most projects and teams still learn security the hard way: after the breach.

**This blog exists to change that.**

I deliver clear, practical, no-fluff education on **Operational Security (OpSec)** , cybersecurity fundamentals, and battle-tested best practices specifically designed for blockchain projects, DAOs, core teams, and builders. Every article, guide, checklist, and case study is written for real teams operating in crypto — not generic corporate advice.

  


**What I cover:**

  * Secure key & wallet management for individuals and multisigs
  * Team OpSec: communication hygiene, access control, device policies, and travel security
  * Threat modeling for DAOs and early-stage projects
  * Privacy tools, anonymity best practices, and opsec-aware development workflows
  * Post-incident recovery, transparency standards, and how to communicate during a crisis
  * Emerging risks (MEV, governance attacks, supply-chain exploits, insider threats)



  


Content is published regularly, freely available, and deliberately accessible — whether you’re a solo founder, a growing protocol team, or a contributor in a large DAO. I turn complex security concepts into actionable steps that teams can implement immediately.

  


Every project that levels up its OpSec reduces the attack surface for everyone. Fewer successful exploits mean stronger user trust, lower insurance costs, healthier token economics, and more capital flowing into public goods instead of recovery efforts.

  


By funding this project you’re not just supporting my blog — you’re investing in **preventative security infrastructure** for web3. Funds will be used transparently to:

  


  * Publish consistent, high-quality educational content
  * Commission expert guest contributions
  * Create downloadable templates, checklists, and toolkits
  * Expand reach through translations, AMAs, and community workshops
  * [DeFi Roadmap 2.0](https://github.com/OffcierCia/DeFi-Developer-Road-Map)



  


Security shouldn’t be a luxury or an afterthought. It should be the default. Help me make that the norm — one informed team at a time.
