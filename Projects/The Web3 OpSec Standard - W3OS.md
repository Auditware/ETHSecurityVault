## Description

## **What this project is**

W3OS is an open-source operational security standard for web3 teams with the goal of making it easier for teams to set up and maintain effective security practices without requiring security expertise.

Most security failures in web3 don’t come from complex smart contract bugs, but from from operational security failures:

  * compromised multi-sig signers
  * misconfigured service permissions
  * phishing and social engineering
  * poor key management
  * vulnerable communications
  * malware infected devices



  


W3OS focuses on addressing these problems with practical, implementation-focused guidance.

GitHub:[ https://github.com/W3OSC/web3-opsec-standard](https://github.com/W3OSC/web3-opsec-standard)

## **Why this matters**

Operational security is one of the most common sources of failure, but industry guidance is not well standardized or easily actionable.

  


As a result, teams often:

  * build ad hoc security processes
  * rely on inconsistent or incomplete guidance
  * improve only after an incident



  


These failures don’t hurt just teams, they impact users, protocols, and the broader ecosystem. W3OS aims to provide a shared, practical baseline that teams can adopt early and iterate on over time to remain secure at any scale.

## **What makes W3OS different**

**Implementation-focused**

Guidance is concrete: what to enable, what to disable, and how to configure systems correctly.

**Organization-focused**

W3OS includes guidance for organizations AND individuals from Web3 startups to VC firms. It is adaptable to any size or type of organization.

**Designed for failure**

Humans make mistakes, processes break down over time, and controls fail. W3OS focuses on adding defense-in-depth with multiple layers of security controls to reduce the impact of eventual vulnerability.

**Standard + tooling**

W3OS is not just docs and guides. We also build tools that help teams enforce these practices and monitor for potential compromise.

## **Current progress**

  * Initial version of the W3OS standard is live and publicly available
  * Extensive set of service configuration guides across commonly used platforms including X, Google, Github, Telegram
  * Coverage of core domains: Wallets & Multi-sig, Endpoints, Communications, DevOps, and General (security management)
  * Early adoption and feedback from teams in the ecosystem, with periodic updates

## **What funding will support**

**Expanding the standard**

  * Broader coverage across infrastructure, wallets, domains, CI/CD, and internal tooling
  * More detailed configurations for commonly used platforms (communication tools, cloud providers, DevOps systems, AI tools, etc.)
  * Improved structure, documentation, and onboarding
  * Screenshots and diagrams to make guides easier to follow



**Building open-source tools**

  * Monitoring tools for high-risk actions (multi-sigs, permissions, domain changes, etc.)
  * Lightweight tooling to help teams enforce best practices
  * Integrations that reduce operational overhead



**Increasing adoption**

  * Clear onboarding and implementation paths for teams
  * Direct support for teams adopting W3OS
  * Establishing W3OS as a recognized baseline for operational security in web3



  


## **Why this is a public good**

W3OS is fully open source and designed to be usable by any team, empowering them to be proactive and take security into their own hands.

  


Improving operational security across web3 reduces preventable incidents, protects users and funds, and raises the baseline for the entire ecosystem.
