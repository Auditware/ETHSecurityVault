## Description

Threat intelligence in crypto is fragmented - scattered across private Telegram chats, Signal groups, and individual researchers' notes. When a new phishing campaign launches or a threat actor targets the ecosystem, critical intel stays siloed while users lose funds. We think the ecosystem needs a better way to share, analyze, and act on threats together.

  


SEAL Intel is a SEAL (Security Alliance) Initiative - SEAL is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit founded by samczsun that serves as neutral security infrastructure for crypto.

  


We operate an ISAC (Information Sharing and Analysis Center) for crypto - a threat intelligence platform where security teams can collect, correlate, and share threat data. Unlike traditional ISACs that charge membership fees, SEAL Intel is completely free. Free to join, free to contribute data, free to consume intelligence and defend your organization. We believe the best way to protect crypto is to remove every barrier to sharing and acting on threat intelligence.

  


Members include security teams from Chainalysis, Ethereum Foundation, Filecoin Foundation, MetaMask, Polygon, Scroll, Uniswap Labs, and many others across the ecosystem.

  


But we don't just collect intelligence - we act on it. SEAL Intel powers a global phishing defense network integrated with MetaMask, Phantom, WalletConnect, and Backpack. When a phishing site is verified, warnings propagate across all participating wallets in real time - a decentralized immune system protecting millions of users.

  


We also publish public advisories on active threat actors targeting crypto. Recent advisories include UNC1069 (DPRK) fake Microsoft Teams and Zoom call campaigns with 164 IOCs, VS Code Tasks Abuse used by DPRK's Contagious Interview operation, SLOVENLY COMET (OTP interception via the SMS supply chain), Secret Backdoor domain hijackings via SMS, ELUSIVE COMET social engineering, reflected XSS exploits by Perpetual Drainer targeting Solana and Tron users, TraderTraitor DPRK threat to crypto exchanges, and PSAs on fake Telegram support channel drainers. Our lazarus.group project tracks and exposes DPRK operatives infiltrating the ecosystem, with 60+ profiled operatives and $6B+ in state-sponsored theft tracked. Data from over 100 professionals and organizations flows through our platform, feeding directly into SEAL Frameworks & SEAL Certifications best practices.

  


Our mission is to make SEAL Intel the central nervous system for crypto security - so that when a new threat emerges, the entire ecosystem can detect, share, and respond before funds are lost.

  


Your donation goes directly toward SEAL Intel - supporting the contributors who run this initiative day to day and the tooling that keeps our work going: threat feeds, phishing defense infrastructure, advisory research and publication, ISAC coordination, and lazarus.group. The biggest line item is operating costs for the analysts, researchers, and engineers doing the work.

  


Note: SEAL Intel is an initiative of SEAL (Security Alliance), a 501(c)(3) nonprofit. Donations made through this Giveth project stay with SEAL Intel and support the contributors and infrastructure dedicated to this initiative - they are not pooled across SEAL's other initiatives.
