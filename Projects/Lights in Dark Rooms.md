## Description

**Lights in Dark Rooms** is a gripping investigative documentary exposing the hidden horrors of Southeast Asia's sprawling scam compounds. In shadowy industrial complexes across Cambodia, Myanmar, and Laos, hundreds of thousands are ensnared in a modern form of slavery—trafficked under false promises of high-paying jobs, only to be forced into relentless online fraud schemes.

  


These "_fraud factories_ " run by transnational crime syndicates—often Chinese-linked—exploit vulnerable people from across Asia and beyond. Victims endure brutal conditions: 17+ hour shifts scamming strangers via romance fraud, pig-butchering crypto cons, and other deceptions that have stolen tens of billions globally. Failure to meet quotas brings torture, electrocution, beatings, and worse. Yet many scammers are themselves captives, trapped in a cycle of coercion and fear.

  


Through undercover footage, survivor testimonies, expert insights, and on-the-ground reporting, the film illuminates the dark rooms where hope is extinguished and human lives become commodities. It reveals how these compounds thrive amid weak governance, armed conflicts, and porous borders, fueling a multibillion-dollar cybercrime empire.

  


**_"Lights in Dark Rooms"_** shines a necessary spotlight on this humanitarian crisis, urging global action to dismantle the networks, rescue the enslaved, and protect future victims from this insidious blend of human trafficking and digital exploitation.

  


Our investigative documentary **Lights In Dark Rooms** shines a light on the dark side of Web3 scams while delivering real impact through charity.
