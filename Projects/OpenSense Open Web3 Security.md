## Description

## **OpenSense is a free Web3 security education project.**

<https://www.youtube.com/@opensensepw>

  


**For the past few years, we’ve been publishing technical content to help auditors, researchers, developers, and curious builders get better at understanding how blockchain systems break and how to secure them.**

  


**So far, the project has reached 36,851 YouTube subscribers** and generated **more than 11,000 hours of lifetime watch time**. 

  


We’ve published content across a wide range of security topics, including smart contract auditing, exploit analysis, fuzzing, formal verification, EVM internals, zero knowledge systems, account abstraction, MEV, vaults, oracles, and protocol design. Our library includes both educational explainers and deep technical conversations with security researchers and auditors.

  


OpenSense is not only a content library. We also built a real community around this work. A lot of our videos were shared directly through Discord announcements, live calls, and community coordination. We’ve used the community to distribute new releases, organize sessions, launch a CTF with rewards, and keep security learning active beyond YouTube itself.

  


The main goal of OpenSense is simple: make high quality security knowledge more open and easier to access. Good security education helps new auditors enter the field, helps existing researchers sharpen their thinking, and helps builders avoid preventable mistakes. We believe that makes the Ethereum and broader Web3 ecosystem stronger.

  


Funding would help us keep producing content, improve editing and distribution, and create more hands on educational experiences for the community.

  


Here’s an even more human version, with a bit more personality:

**OpenSense exists to help more people get good at Web3 security.**

**We’ve spent the last few years publishing free educational content for auditors, researchers, and developers who want to understand smart contract security at a deeper level.**

  


**The project has grown into a meaningful learning hub, with 36,851 YouTube subscribers** and **11,000+ hours of lifetime watch time**. Along the way, we’ve covered topics like auditing, exploit breakdowns, fuzzing, formal verification, EVM internals, ZK, account abstraction, MEV, oracles, and other areas that matter for real world blockchain security.

  


A big part of OpenSense has also happened through community. We’ve shared releases through Discord, brought people into live sessions, launched security challenges, and used the community as a place where people can keep learning together instead of just watching passively.

  


We see OpenSense as public goods work because better security knowledge helps everyone. It helps new people enter the field, helps experienced people keep improving, and helps the ecosystem become safer over time.

  


Funding would help us continue and expand that work.
