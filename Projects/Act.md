## Description

## **act is a formal specification language and verification framework for Ethereum smart contracts, and rigorous tool for ensuring smart contract safety.  **

  


Originally developed as part of the Ethereum Foundation's formal verification efforts, act has found a permanent home at [Argot](https://www.argot.org/), an independent non-profit organization that stewards critical Ethereum infrastructure. With Argot also being home to Solidity, act is well positioned to contribute to the specification of Core Solidity in the future.

  


At its core, act bridges the gap between human-readable specifications and EVM bytecode, proving their equivalence using [hevm ](https://github.com/argotorg/hevm)as its underlying execution engine. This makes it possible to formally verify that a contract's implementation does exactly what its specification says it should,**across multiple smart contract languages** , including Solidity and Vyper. Rather than relying on testing alone, act gives development teams a mathematically grounded way to reason about contract behavior, making it a powerful tool for high-assurance deployments. On top of that, the project has recently been enhanced with an **output to the Lean prover** , making verification usable across multiple platforms. 

  


## Recent work: 

Recent work has focused on building act's theoretical foundations from the ground up, formalizing its mathematical semantics and establishing proofs of type safety. With these foundations in place, the team launched its act v0.2.0 release: <https://github.com/argotorg/act/releases/tag/v0.2.0>

  


This release is an important milestone for the project, marking the transition from early-stage research to a tool that developers can begin to explore and build familiarity with. As work on **Core Solidity** matures the team will begin exploratory work to verify smart contracts written in Core Solidity.
