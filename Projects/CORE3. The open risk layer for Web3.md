## Description

# The open‑source risk layer for Web3. Free for everyone.

Our goal is to boost transparency, accountability, and security – and to grow the Web3 cybersecurity market as a whole.

## We already did this once

We built CER.live: open security ratings for 290 exchanges, trusted by CoinGecko as 20% of their exchange trust score. Used by millions. Free. CORE3 is the same idea applied to the whole web3 ecosystem - protocols, DeFi, chains, governance.

  


[https://CORE3.io](https://core3.io) is live today. No paywall. 1,400+ projects analyzed and openly available.

  


## The market still fails to answer one question

Audit badge - enough. Clean UI - enough. Stable yield - enough.

  


That is why weak, fragile, and negligent projects still attract capital.

Opacity is profitable.

The current system rewards projects that market security better than they practice it - and bad actors benefit directly from users not seeing what sits underneath.

  


**As long as risk stays invisible, responsibility is optional and hype stays competitive.**

  


## What everyone can see now

Each project gets a Probability of Loss (0-100) built from six categories where failures actually happen: financial structure, governance, dependencies, operations, reputation, and technical stack.

  


Not an audit badge. Not a dashboard metric. A forward-looking signal built on open methodology - open for review, open for challenge, open for improvement by community.

  


0–20 > relatively stable.

20–50 > meaningful structural risk.

50+ > high likelihood of loss.

  


## What we see across the market

We analyzed 1,400+ projects. Around 80% show structural risk signals. Even top-tier names are not automatically safe. Loss is not the exception - hidden risk is the norm.

  


## The human layer on top of data

Numbers alone aren't enough. CORE3 adds Proof of Voice - a public expert layer: structured opinions on projects, verifiable track records, community challenge and refinement. So «I hope it's safe» becomes «I understand why it's risky». And that judgment is public, accountable, and improvable by anyone.

  


## What your contribution does

Every dollar expands coverage.

More projects analyzed, more risks made visible before someone funds them.

Every project added to the open dataset is one fewer blind spot for the next person entering Web3.

  


In quadratic funding, the number of people who contribute matters as much as the amount. If you've ever lost money to a project that looked safe, or want to make sure fewer people do, this is the contribution that makes that outcome less likely.
