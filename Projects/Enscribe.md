## Description

Enscribe replaces risky hex addresses with trusted, human-readable identities for smart contracts, wallets, and agents. 

##   


## What

  


Enscribe provides identity infrastructure for onchain teams, powered by ENS.

  


Today most onchain Ethereum infrastructure including contracts, wallets, agents are identified via hex addresses. This creates significant security risk, as users must trust they are interacting with the correct contract or wallet.

  


Enscribe makes it easy for team to assign human-readable ENS names and associated metadata to their entire onchain infrastructure using .eth names or using DNS names, via DNS on ENS.

  


Enscribe provides the tooling teams need to design, deploy, and manage these namespaces in production, including multi-user workspaces, API access, and integrations with tools like Safe.

  


The end result of using Enscribe, is onchain identity for smart contracts and wallets. So instead of simply a hex address, users can view rich identity information using Enscribe, such as demonstrated below.

  


See in the Enscribe App [here](https://app.enscribe.xyz/explore/1/v2.app.enscribe.eth).

  


## Why

Address spoofing and poisoning attacks are responsible for tens of millions of losses every year. These are preventable with proper onchain identities for contracts and wallets.

  


Enscribe reduces this risk by:

  * Replacing opaque hex addresses, with human-readable ENS names
  * Help standardise how protocols present their onchain presence
  * Make it easy for wallets, explorers and users to verify these assets
  * Provide identify workflows that teams can use to ensure they incorporate good identity hygene into their products and protocols



  


This makes the Ethereum ecosystem safer by default and reduces the attack surface for phishing and spoofing.

  


## Where

Enscribe is built on Ethereum and integrates directly with ENS. Our app is available at app.enscribe.xyz, and we’re collaborating with the ENS DAO and other algiend projects to drive broad adoption.

  


We've already integrated with Safe, Ethereum Follow Protocol and Open Labels Initiative, and provide verification support via Etherscan, Blockscout and Sourcify.

  


  


We've also collaborated with a number of notable projects on their naming including Nouns, Cork, Liquity, Giveth, SSV Network, Superfluid and Kleros. You can learn more about some of our partnerships on our [blog](https://www.enscribe.xyz/blog/tags/partnerships).

  


## How

  


 \- Over the past year, Enscribe has helped protocols including Nouns DAO, Liquity, Cork, Giveth, Based Nouns, and SSV Network adopt ENS-based naming for production smart contracts.

 \- We launched and continued growing [ENS Contract Naming Season](https://www.enscribe.xyz/blog/contract-naming-season), turning contract identity into a visible, incentivised ecosystem initiative.

 \- We expanded Enscribe from a contract naming tool into identity infrastructure for onchain teams, covering contracts, wallets, multisigs, and agents.

 \- We shipped richer identity tooling, including contract metadata, Name Explorer, and better ways to browse and understand namespaces.

 \- We improved ecosystem visibility through integrations such as Blockscout contract identity scoring.

 \- We published API documentation, community calls, and video tutorials to make ENS-based identity easier for developers and teams to adopt.

 \- We continued shipping practical tooling including Safe support, batch naming, and developer workflows for Foundry and Hardhat.

  


Enscribe is also aligned with [Vitalik's vision on low-risk DeFi.](https://x.com/VitalikButerin/status/1970820664853741959)
