## Description

Everyone gets rekt eventually. Unless they learn from those who already did.

  


**Rekt News** is the leading independent investigative journalism publication covering security in DeFi and the broader Ethereum ecosystem. Since 2020, we've operated as a public good: no paywall, no token, no VC funding. We produce in-depth post-mortem analyses of exploits, original investigative features, documentary films, and a full-scale annual security conference. Everything is freely available, always.

  


🔗 Website: <https://rekt.news> 🔗 Security Hub: <https://hub.rekt.news> 🔗 Summit: <https://summit.rekt.news> 🔗 GitHub: <https://github.com/RektHQ> 🔗 YouTube: [https://www.youtube.com/@RektNews.](https://www.youtube.com/@RektNews./videos) 🔗 X/Twitter: <https://x.com/RektHQ>

  


  


  


  


**What We Do**

**1\. Investigative Post-Mortems & Pre-Mortems**

When a protocol gets exploited, Rekt publishes detailed breakdowns tracing the attack vector, identifying the root cause, crediting the researchers who spotted it, and documenting the response. Our Rekt Leaderboard (<https://rekt.news/leaderboard>) tracks cumulative DeFi losses and serves as a live reference for the ecosystem.

  


In the past 12 months alone we've covered: the ByBit $1.4B Lazarus heist, Drift Protocol's $285M DPRK social engineering attack, Cetus's $223M math exploit on Sui, Balancer's $128M rounding exploit across multiple chains, the Resolv Labs $25M private key compromise, Aave's $27.7M oracle misconfiguration liquidations, the GMX $42M reentrancy exploit (coordinated disclosure, $5M bounty paid), Step Finance's $27.3M executive inbox compromise, and dozens more. We cover not just exploits but systemic issues: vibe-coded smart contracts (the Moonwell oracle exploit may be the first major exploit of AI-generated code), DPRK infiltration of security circles ("The Impersonator"), centralization risk ("We Have a Centralization Issue" on AWS outages taking down crypto infrastructure), and governance attacks ("Code Is Law but Voting Is War").

  


We don't just cover hacks after they happen. Our "House of Cards" investigation (October 2025) exposed the recursive minting loop between Stream Finance (xUSD) and Elixir Network (deUSD) before the depeg occurred. We traced on-chain how $1.9M in USDC was leveraged into $14.5M in synthetic stablecoins. When the collapse came in November ("The Loop Contagion"), $93 million vanished and xUSD crashed 77%. We published the warning first.

  


🔗 House of Cards: <https://rekt.news/house-of-cards> 🔗 Leaderboard: <https://rekt.news/leaderboard>

**Why This Matters**

When a protocol is exploited, the lessons are there for everyone, but only if someone documents them rigorously, independently, and accessibly. Rekt fills that gap. We're the ecosystem's post-incident learning infrastructure and, increasingly, its pre-incident early warning system.

  


Security education at scale is a public good that's chronically underfunded. Audit firms have business models. Bug bounty platforms have business models. That doesn't fit a traditional business model.
