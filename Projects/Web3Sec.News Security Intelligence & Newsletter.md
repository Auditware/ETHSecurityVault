## Description

[Web3Sec News](https://web3sec.news) is one of Asia's largest Web3 security communities and newsletters, founded in April 2023 by Raiders ([@__Raiders](https://x.com/__Raiders)), Ethereum Foundation grantee, SEAL Alliance contributor, and published MITRE AADAPT researcher.

  


With 10,000+ members across Telegram (2.4k subs), WhatsApp, Discord, LinkedIn, and X, and 2,000+ subscribers on Substack, Web3Sec News has grown into a trusted security intelligence hub for developers, researchers, and protocol teams across the Ethereum ecosystem.

  


## Web3Sec News delivers:

\- Weekly security newsletter covering the latest hacks, vulnerabilities, and threat intelligence across Ethereum and L2s

\- 120+ published hack postmortems and exploit breakdowns covering real incidents in depth

\- Deep-dive technical research on protocols including Uniswap v4, Balancer, Aave, and more

\- Security awareness content distributed across 5+ channels reaching 10,000+ readers

\- Community-driven threat discussions, alerts, and real-time security updates

\- Educational content on DNS security, supply chain attacks, phishing, and non-smart-contract attack vectors that most security resources ignore

  


## Why?

Most Web3 security content is either too technical for everyday users or too surface-level for serious researchers. Web3Sec News fills the middle ground: rigorous enough to be featured in BlockThreat and cited by researchers, accessible enough to shift baseline security awareness across thousands of developers and users.

  


Security education compounds. A single well-distributed postmortem or threat alert doesn't just inform one person. It shifts how thousands of developers think about security decisions, which protocols they trust, and how they build. That multiplier effect is undervalued and underfunded in the current security ecosystem.

  


The reality is that most exploits in Web3 today stem from non-smart-contract vectors: phishing, DNS hijacks, supply chain compromises, social engineering. Web3Sec News consistently covers these blind spots when most security media ignores them.

  


**Global reach with a strong focus on Asia.** Distributed across Telegram, WhatsApp, Discord, LinkedIn, and X. Primary audience spans India, Southeast Asia, and the broader Ethereum developer and researcher community worldwide.

Web3Sec News runs as an independent, community-first initiative. Content is researched, written, and distributed by Raiders with support from ecosystem sponsors and partners who believe in accessible security education as a public good.
