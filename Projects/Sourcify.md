## Description

## **Sourcify is an open-source, public-good smart contract verification service built on the principle that verified Ethereum contract datasets should be open, accessible, and owned by no single entity.**

  


Originally part of the Ethereum Foundation and now stewarded by Argot, Sourcify has long been an advocate for security and open standards in the ecosystem.Its mission is straightforward but critical:

  


> **ensuring that the link between deployed bytecode and human-readable source code is verifiable by anyone, and that the verification infrastructure passes the walkaway test: no single actor's disappearance should compromise the integrity or availability of the data.**

  


At a practical level, Sourcify makes Ethereum safer by giving users, developers, and tools the ability to inspect exactly what a smart contract does before interacting with it. When a contract is verified through Sourcify, its source code is publicly available and matched to its on-chain bytecode, making contract behavior transparent and independently confirmable. This transparency is foundational to a healthier ecosystem, and Sourcify reinforces it by operating as a credibly neutral public good rather than a commercial service.

  


The team has spent the past period building the technical foundations of a reliable and scalable verification service and currently supports contracts written in Solidity, Vyper, and Fe. As those foundations mature, focus is increasingly shifting toward outreach and community initiatives, including active participation in the [Verifier Alliance](https://verifieralliance.org/), a collaborative effort to ensure verified contract data is consistently available across explorers and verifiers, and to publish this dataset as a shared, open resource for the entire ecosystem (you can also find more info under [https://one-trillion-dollar-security.netlify.app/](https://one-trillion-dollar-security.netlify.app) under Communtiy).

  


**Sourcify is also playing an active role in tackling one of Ethereum's most persistent UX and security failures: blind signing.** For years, users confirming transactions have been confronted with raw, incomprehensible hex data, with no reliable way to confirm that what they are signing matches their actual intent. Sourcify is participating in the ERC-7730 Clear Signing working group and developing tooling and an SDK to help protocols publish human-readable transaction descriptors, ensuring that the infrastructure behind clear signing remains open, neutral, and verifiable. It is work that sits squarely at the intersection of open-source integrity, security, and real-world user protection.

  


## How donations will be used:

100% of raised funds go directly toward the salaries of the three engineers maintaining Sourcify and delivering on its [roadmap](https://www.argot.org/blog/2026-01-15-argot-roadmap-update-2026-1#sourcify) over the coming months. This includes completing the migration to API v2, expanding contract coverage through similarity verification across all supported chains, advancing the Verifier Alliance's open dataset with improved Parquet exports and data sanitization, and contributing development capacity to the ERC-7730 Clear Signing initiative, including SDK development and tooling for generating specification files. Funds also support ongoing collaboration with the broader Ethereum ecosystem, including ethereum-lists/chains maintenance and Solidity infrastructure work.
