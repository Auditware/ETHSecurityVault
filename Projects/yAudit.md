## Description

**yAudit** (formerly yAcademy) is a security collective focused on smart contracts and zero-knowledge systems: auditing, research, and increasingly, tooling.

  


**Our core values:**

  * Rigour
  * Meritocracy
  * Labour is Equity
  * Public goods are good



  


**The Fellowship**

Noticing a sizeable gap in security-oriented expertise across the Ethereum ecosystem, we created the yAcademy Fellowship, a month-long, public-good bootcamp designed to let grassroots security researchers deepen their skills and experience what a real security engagement looks like.

Each cohort, roughly 20 to 25 fellows execute security reviews alongside expert resident auditors. Clients get a high number of additional eyes on their codebase at no cost; fellows gain hands-on, applicable experience. Top performers are then invited to join yAudit as resident auditors and continue delivering professional audit services.

To date, we have run nine fellowship blocks (seven focused on smart contract security and two on zero-knowledge systems), training a total of 120 fellows. Many of these alumni have gone on to become active contributors in the broader Ethereum security ecosystem, whether as yAudit resident auditors or as independent researchers and auditors elsewhere. The fellowship remains one of the few structured, merit-based pathways into professional blockchain security that operates entirely as a public good.

  


**Track record**

We launched in late 2021 to help make DeFi more secure. Since then, yAudit has completed hundreds of security reviews for major DeFi protocols, securing over $2 billion in total value locked.

  


**Expanding into zero-knowledge security**

In 2024, we expanded into ZK security and research, driven by three converging trends: the SNARKification of the Ethereum protocol itself, the proliferation of ZK-powered networks, dApps, and cross-chain middleware, and the growing role of ZK in hardening Web2 through primitives like zkEmail and MPC-TLS ("zkTLS").

  


**How your donation helps**

All funds donated through Giveth will go directly toward sustaining and expanding the yAudit Fellowship. Running each fellowship block involves coordinating resident auditors, sourcing real-world codebases for review, and providing structured mentorship over an intensive month-long engagement. Your contributions help us keep this program free for fellows, maintain the quality of mentorship, and ultimately grow the pipeline of skilled security researchers that the Ethereum ecosystem urgently needs. Every donation, regardless of size, strengthens the public-good infrastructure that makes DeFi and ZK systems safer for everyone.
