## Description

**What We Do**

CypherTalk is a **freely accessible, independently produced podcast** dedicated to cybersecurity, privacy, and the human side of staying safe in a digital world. Every episode is openly available on major podcast platforms (including Spotify and Apple Podcasts). A separate YouTube channel complements the audio format with short clips and longer educational content — **no paywalls and no pay-to-speak model**.

_Our mission is simple: make advanced security topics accessible and actionable for Web3 builders, researchers, and anyone navigating today's threat landscape. No hype. Just signal._

  


**Ethereum Ecosystem Relevance**

CypherTalk is deeply rooted in the Ethereum security community. Co-host **Dr. Stefan Beyer** has been auditing Ethereum smart contracts since 2017, with **100+ audits** completed across leading firms. He has contributed to Ethereum security standards, including roles in professional certification programs and conference teaching.

Beyond the hosts, our guest roster reflects the same depth of Ethereum involvement:

  * **Griff Green** — co-founder of Giveth.io and a key force behind TheDAO Security Fund itself — discussing the history of the DAO hack, the evolution of smart contract auditing, and quadratic funding for security
  * **Jordi Baylina** — founder of ZisK — exploring ZK proof security, privacy-first development, and circuit auditing
  * **Peter Kacherginsky** — founder of BlockThreat — on Web3 threat intelligence, attack pattern analysis, and operational security for teams managing significant on-chain value



CypherTalk directly addresses the security of everyday Ethereum users and builders — from zero-trust architecture and phishing defense for distributed Web3 teams (_Operational Security_ episode) to real-world cryptographic vulnerabilities in tools like password managers and E2E encryption systems (_Password Manager Security and Applied Cryptography_ episode).

  


**Content & Track Record**

CypherTalk publishes new episodes every two weeks, with a growing library of conversations covering:

  * The evolving threat landscape: social engineering, phishing, supply chain attacks
  * Operational security for distributed and Web3 teams
  * ZK proof security, implementation risks, and privacy-first design
  * Web3 threat intelligence and attack pattern analysis
  * The history and future of Ethereum security funding
  * Applied cryptography: password manager vulnerabilities, E2E encryption flaws, formal verification



 _All episodes are verifiable and publicly accessible. This is working, published content — not a concept._

  


**Open Access & Public Goods Commitment**

CypherTalk operates as a public good. All content is released under a the [**Creative Commons Attribution (CC BY) 4.0 International license**](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/4.0/deed.en) and will remain freely accessible. We are committed to maintaining open access after any funding received and will not transition to a restricted model.

  * **$5,000–$10,000** — Equipment and production for an extended season of episodes, improved audio/video quality
  * **$10,000–$25,000** — Extended production, professional video production for longer-form educational content, conference attendance as media

**Why This Matters**

**Security education is infrastructure.** Builders who understand OpSec, ZK risks, and smart contract vulnerabilities make better decisions — and fewer costly mistakes. CypherTalk fills a gap between highly technical research papers and surface-level content, reaching the people who need this knowledge most.
