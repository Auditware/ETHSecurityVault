## Description

We are a small Ethereum security team focused on one of the hardest problems in DeFi: **stopping exploits while they are happening**.

The project was started by [@snfernandez](https://x.com/snfernandez) and [@feliam](https://x.com/feliam), and later joined by [@0xlucca](https://x.com/0xlucca) to continue and expand the mission. We have been doing this since 2024, with no recurring revenue beyond bounties paid by the projects we rescued.

  


## What we do

Most crypto security work happens before or after the attack. We focus on the few seconds in the middle, when funds can still be saved.

Almost nobody operates in the narrow window where an attack is already live and funds can still be saved.﻿

We build and operate an automated whitehat security agent for Ethereum and L2s. The system monitors the chain in real time, detects exploit patterns, validates rescue paths, and executes protective transactions fast enough to secure vulnerable assets before they are stolen.

This is not a research idea or an unfinished prototype. It is working security infrastructure used in real incidents.

  


## Progress so far

Since starting in 2024, our whitehat operations have rescued **more than $1.5 million in user and protocol funds**.

That includes $1 million in the **Balancer rescue** alone in less than 12 seconds after the attacker’s first move ([writeup](https://bitfinding.com/blog/balancer-exploit-interception)).

Other public incident reports also include multiple rescues on Ethereum, Arbitrum, BSC and Base showing the same live interception approach in production ([blog](https://bitfinding.com/blog)).

We were also the first recipients of a confirmed Safe Harbor bounty, helping validate SEAL’s Safe Harbor framework through real exploit interception and fund return. [Safe Harbor](https://frameworks.securityalliance.org/safe-harbor/overview) is SEAL’s framework for pre-authorizing whitehat interventions during active exploits.

  


## Why this matters

Ethereum already has many strong auditors, researchers, and security platforms. That part of the ecosystem is active and well developed. What is still rare is active exploit interception.

Safe Harbor exists because live whitehat intervention is a distinct need: it gives protocols a framework to pre-authorize independent whitehats to step in during active exploits, rescue funds, and return them safely.

  


## How it works

Our system continuously watches Ethereum for patterns associated with active exploitation.

When a high-confidence threat is detected, it reconstructs the likely attack path, checks whether a rescue transaction can succeed, and intervenes when the conditions are right.

Speed matters, but precision matters too. It is not enough to notice suspicious activity. The system has to act correctly, in real time, under pressure.

Each real incident also improves our heuristics and execution logic, making the system stronger as attack patterns evolve.

## **Where we are going**

Our goal is simple: keep building a reliable live-response security layer for Ethereum.

We want to prove that Ethereum security should not stop at passive monitoring or retrospective analysis. When an attack starts, there should be a real chance to intervene and save funds.
