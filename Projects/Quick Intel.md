## Description

**_The free, open token security scanner protecting Web3 traders across 60+ chains._**

  


Quick Intel is a free, public good token security scanner that protects everyday users from rug pulls, honeypots, and malicious smart contracts before they trade.

  


Anyone can paste a contract address into [app.quickintel.io](https://app.quickintel.io), pick a chain, and get a real-time, AI-driven breakdown of exactly what that contract can do, whether it can mint, blacklist, pause transfers, set arbitrary fees, drain liquidity, steal tokens from your wallet, or hide behind a proxy. No wallet connection. No paywall. No signup. The same scan a professional auditor would run, available in seconds to anyone, anywhere.

  


**Why this matters and why I built it**

I started Quick Intel after losing money to scam tokens in 2022. That experience made one thing painfully clear: most users have no way to read a smart contract, and the tooling that _could_ protect them was either paywalled, closed-source, limited to one or two chains, or hidden behind APIs only big platforms could afford. Meanwhile, scammers were industrializing and deploying thousands of malicious tokens a day across every new EVM chain that launched.

  


Token level scams are still one of the largest sources of preventable user loss in crypto. Phishing sites, drainer approvals, and social engineering get most of the attention, but the contract itself is often the weapon. If we can show users the weapon before they pull the trigger, most of these scams stop working. That's the entire mission of Quick Intel, to make sure no one ever has to lose what I and many others lost, because the information was right there if only someone had built the tool to surface it.

  


**What Quick Intel does today**

  * **60+ blockchains supported** — Ethereum, Base, Arbitrum, Optimism, Polygon, BNB Chain, Avalanche, Solana, and every major L2/L3, plus long-tail and niche chains most scanners ignore.
  * **60M+ tokens scanned** across the ecosystem.
  * **4M+ scams detected and flagged** — honeypots, hidden mints, allowance bypasses, transferFee backdoors, tx.origin wrappers, SafeMath manipulation, proxy traps, and more.
  * **15M+ API calls per week** served to developers, wallets, bots, and trading platforms.
  * **Free public scanner** at [app.quickintel.io](https://app.quickintel.io), no wallet, no account, no fee.
  * **Developer APIs** documented at [developer.quickintel.io](https://developer.quickintel.io) so any builder can embed token security into their own product.



  


**Who already relies on Quick Intel**

Quick Intel powers token security on platforms used by millions of traders every day, including **DexTools, DexScreener, GeckoTerminal, and Gate.io** , plus a long list of bots, wallets, and dashboards. When a chart screen warns you that a token can mint or has a buy/sell tax, there's a good chance that signal came from Quick Intel.

  


I've also been pushing the frontier on detection itself: advanced allowance-bypass scam detection (catching tx.origin wrappers, SafeMath manipulation tricks, transferFee backdoors), worker-thread-parallelized honeypot simulation, and a fully self hostable detection pipeline. The goal is for the _next_ class of scam to get caught the same week it appears, not six months later after thousands of users have already lost funds.

  


**Why this is a public good for Ethereum**

Ethereum's promise is a permissionless market, but a permissionless market only works if users can verify what they're trading. Closed, paywalled, or single chain scanners create an information asymmetry that scammers exploit. Quick Intel exists so that _every_ trader, on _every_ EVM chain, has the same level of protection that institutional players have, for free.

  


**How donations will be used**

100% of QF funds go directly into keeping Quick Intel free, fast, and ahead of attackers:

  * **Subsidize free scans for end users** — every API call costs money. Donations directly offset the cost of keeping the scanner free for the traders who need it most.
  * **Expand chain coverage** — onboarding new EVM chains, L2s, and emerging ecosystems as they launch (often before scammers arrive).
  * **Improve scam detection** — funding ongoing R&D into new detection signatures (allowance bypasses, novel proxy traps, MEV-aware honeypots, and the next class of scam we haven't seen yet).
  * **Free security education** — articles, threads, and developer guides on how to read a contract and recognize red flags, so users can protect themselves long-term.
  * **Open developer access** — creating a developer tier free for indie builders and public-good projects so wallets, dashboards, and trading tools can integrate security by default.
