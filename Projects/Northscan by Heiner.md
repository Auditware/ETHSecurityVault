## Description

Hey! I am Heiner, an independent cyber threat intelligence researcher specializing in on-chain investigations. Since 2024, my work has concentrated on two closely related threat vectors: crypto fraud infrastructure and North Korean state-sponsored IT worker operations. My methodology combines on-chain fund flow analysis, open-source intelligence, and Human Intelligence evidence-based reports. All findings are published openly and made freely available to the security community.

  


**Since early 2024**

  


I began following Omega Pro which is a Ponzi Scheme linked to an Ethereum address that later was flagged by OFAC as connected to the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps Quds Force (IRGC-QF): <https://medium.com/coinmonks/omegapro-funds-flow-linked-to-ofac-sdn-address-associated-with-the-irgc-qods-force-irgc-qf-948fd569af46>

  


Around that same time, the crypto ecosystem was getting hit hard by drainers. Names like Monkey Drainer, Pxssy Drainer and Pink Drainer were everywhere. That is where my Pink Drainer investigation comes in. I followed the money, mapped the fraud-as-a-service infrastructure they were running, and through on-chain evidence managed to identify an individual strongly connected to the organization: <https://medium.com/coinmonks/scam-as-a-service-pink-drainer-5b4165371916>

  


**On the North Korean IT worker side, what started as a curiosity turned into a full research track**

  


My interest in DPRK IT Workers started when i was approached multiple times by recruiters through LinkedIn, and something felt off. That curiosity turned into research. The following investigations form a growing body of research into North Korean IT workers operating in GitHub as a platform for persona construction, social engineering, and job infiltration

  


• **Fraud Alert: Fake Recruiters on GitHub and LinkedIn.** This investigation revealed a fraud campaign involving fake recruiters operating on GitHub, making use of LinkedIn Premium accounts: [medium.com/coinmonks/fake-recruiters-in-github](https://medium.com/coinmonks/fake-recruiters-in-github-68d0d3bf297d)

• **Suspicious Activity in GitHub Associated with DPRK IT Workers.** An investigation mapping suspicious GitHub patterns tied to DPRK IT Workers targeting developers and companies: [medium.com/coinmonks/suspicious-activity-in-github](https://medium.com/coinmonks/suspicious-activity-in-github-associated-with-lazarus-group-200868dff910)

• **The SuperStar Campaign: Reviewing GitHub Accounts Linked to DPRK IT Workers.** This report documented a coordinated network of GitHub personas used for social engineering, named the SuperStar Campaign. It mapped the cluster's reach, account construction patterns, and tactics used to build credibility within open-source communities: [medium.com/coinmonks/reviewing-github-accounts-lazarus](https://medium.com/coinmonks/reviewing-the-activity-of-github-accounts-associated-with-lazarus-635cb47881dc)

• **Summary of Findings on Suspicious GitHub Activity Linked to DPRK IT Workers.** A consolidated intelligence summary drawing together findings from multiple investigations, structured for use by security teams, OSS maintainers, and policy researchers: <https://medium.com/coinmonks/recap-of-findings-regarding-suspicious-lazarus-activity-on-github-cc361074bdc2>

• **Unmasking Suspicious GitHub Activity: The Wagemole Campaign and Its Links to DPRK Threat Actors.** An investigation into a DPRK-linked cluster of GitHub accounts engaged in systematic identity construction and job infiltration across open-source communities, named the Wagemole Campaign for its method of embedding operatives as legitimate contributors: <https://medium.com/coinmonks/unmasking-suspicious-github-activity-the-wagemole-campaign-and-its-links-to-dprk-threat-actors-248e7901de38>

**Why Northscan.co ?**

  


The activity around this threat vector kept growing, thus I decided to build something more permanent. That is how Northscan was born. Northscan is the platform I created to publish structured, evidence-based threat intelligence on DPRK IT worker operations. All reports are public and free, and the platform runs entirely as an independent effort, without institutional backing or commercial sponsorship.
